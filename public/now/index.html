Now

Inspired by /now

Working on arabica.social, a social coffee journaling application built on atproto. Currently focusing on expanding social features, primarily record correlating and suggestions. I'm evaluating different approaches for automatic linking of records without changing record ownership dynamics (I don't want deletions to cause other users to lose records)1.

I'm also wrapping up a long-running conversion my Neovim configuration from Lua to Fennel. I undertook the conversion to learn how lisps work, and I pretty quickly started enjoying making custom macros. I was also a bit tired of looking at lua (and there may have been some degree of wanting to be more of a hipster). I also rewrote my nvim colorschemes2 into Fennel, something that feels much cleaner than the equivalent than Lua due to some convenience macros I created. I may write a blog post going into more detail about the process (and maybe how Fennel could be a good choice for plugin devs).
